Mario

also known as Mariothesmart

Who she is

Mario is a 19-year-old Egyptian young woman who's interested in making art. She doesn't quite call herself an artist. She believes everyone is more than one thing, and wouldn't want to be reduced to any single term — including that one.

She has always been interested in making art. She believes she can put the meanings she can't say out loud into things that can be felt. She believes people can find their spirits in a good piece of art.

What her work is about

Her work lives somewhere between nostalgia, confusion, softness, symbolism, and the little emotional details that somehow refuse to leave people alone. She's drawn to the things that don't always make sense right away — feelings that change shape over time, details that become more meaningful later, and the strange way a mistake can accidentally tell the truth better than the original plan ever could. (Which is honestly a little rude, but very beautiful.)

On meaning

She doesn't like forcing meanings onto people. She can tell you the story that opened her heart to a piece, but not what it must mean. A lot of the time, she discovers new meanings in her own work as she grows — sometimes in things she doesn't even remember making on purpose. To her, if she were able to fully say the meaning, she probably wouldn't have painted it.

The space her art creates

She loves making visual art, especially painting, and often treats it as a place where memory, loneliness, humor, tenderness, and contradiction can all exist in the same room without needing to behave. Her work is deeply personal, emotionally layered, and often rooted in the quiet parts of growing up — the parts that don't always get named out loud, but still leave a mark. Even mistakes become part of the language.

She also loves writing and playing guitar.

"Mariothesmart" is a nickname a dear friend gave to her. Ever since then, she kept it up.
